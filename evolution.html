Cybersecurity in India

We trace the evolution of telecommunications and computing in India starting from the 1960s, the expansion of telecommunications infrastructure, the role of ICTs in national security and development, and the gradual ascent of cybersecurity as a security apparatus, and ICT policy deliberations. There is an increase in the number and frequency of recent data breaches on Indian organizations across all sectors leaving them vulnerable to cyberattacks. According to the 2019 Cost of a Data Breach report, India had the largest average number of breached records along with the Middle East and the US. Prioritizing cybersecurity and the security of its major sectors is crucial for ensuring India's stature as one of the world's leading investment hubs. Cybersecurity measures both from the government and organizations are expected to evolve and to become more pronounced in the future.

Cyber Laws

Some laws create rules for how individuals and companies may use computers and the internet, while some laws protect people from becoming victims of crime through unethical activities on the internet. According to Ministry of Electronic and Information Technology, Government of India : "Cyber Laws yields legal recognition to electronic documents and a structure to support e-filing and e-commerce transactions and also provides a legal structure to reduce, check cyber crimes"

Here are few areas of the cyber law:

Fraud: Consumers depend on cyber laws to protect them from online fraud. Laws are made to prevent identity theft, credit card theft and other financial crimes that happen online.
Copyright: The internet has made copyright violations easier. In early days of online communication, copyright violations was too easy. Both companies and individuals need lawyers to bring actions to impose copyright protections.
Defamation: Several personnel use the internet to speak their mind. When people use the internet to say things that are not true, it can cross the line into defamation.
Harassment and Stalking: Sometimes online statements can violate criminal laws that forbid harassment and stalking. When a person makes threatening statements again and again about someone else online, there is violation of both civil and criminal laws.
Freedom of Speech: Freedom of speech is an important area of cyber law. Even though cyber laws forbid certain behaviors online, freedom of speech laws also allow people to speak their minds.
