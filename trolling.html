What is Trolling ?

The word trolling is originated from an internet slang "troll". A troll is a person who tries to intentionally upset or hurt people by posting extraneous and inflammatory messages in the comment sections of an online platform such as social medias, chat rooms or blogs.
Trolls post such messages with an intent of provoking emotional responses out of people and interrupting a civil discussion for the sake of their amusement.

Why do people Troll ?

Trolls are the people who get satisfaction by making people upset or degrading them. They have high levels of negative potency which gives them this sadistic nature. And that is why the internet is the best place for them as they can make antagonistic remarks on others by hiding behind their screens with a fake username or userid's which have no connections to their real world identity and hence they do not have to face any real world consequences due to their anonimity.

How do you know that you're being trolled ?

It may be hard at first to identify trolls, but a general giveaway that you are dealing with a troll is that they will constantly try to attack you with negative comments if they find out about something that you like or agree with or insult your tastes and talk about something which is unrelated to the topic of discussion.



How to deal with trolls or how to handle them ?

So now that we are familiar with what trolling is and how to identify trolls or wether you are being trolled, let us see what you should do if you ever get trolled online.
The best way to deal with a troll is to ignore them completely. Do not give any attention or engage in an argument with them because this is exactly what they are looking for, your attention. Trolls are people who are going through some emotional or mental distress in their personal life and so to make themselves feel better they try and take out their anger on you.
If their trolling is abusive or offensive in any manner, you can delete their comments and block them from your social media account and report them. The reporting method varies on different sites or platforms you're using.
