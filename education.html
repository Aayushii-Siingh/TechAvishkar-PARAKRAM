Education institutions need to make cyber-education a priority.

All education institutions hold information on both the staff that work in the school and the children that are taught there. Information ranging from medical history, home addresses, phone numbers and bank details. If a virus penetrates that system then this information is at risk, and could be encrypted, stolen or sold and used by criminals. And it might come of as a surprise to some of you, but the education industry is a prime target for malicious hackers. While threats in this sector are on the rise, many education institutions are not prepared for a cyber-attack nor do they know how to recover from one.

Importance

Education institutions need to make cybersecurity a priority. It's an unfortunate fact that, while cybersecurity in Education is necessary to protect against financial loss and prevent disruption, it's also crucial to protect students from harm.

Education is supposed to be an enlightening experience where exchange of ideas and information takes place. A subject or even a few organised workshops can help students go a long way and help them surf the internet in a safer manner, as the whole world is going digital during current pandemic and as more information migrates into a digital form, school districts have to find the right balance between securing their information infrastructure and making information available to the school community. Not only do administrators have to worry about their own devices, but students must stay informed data coming from outside sources.
While cyber security may seem like it should be the technology department responsibility, the key to great security is to join the departments and have them become your allies in keeping your organization safe.
