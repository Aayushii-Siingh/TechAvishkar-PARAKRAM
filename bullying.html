What is Cyber Bullying ?

Cyberbullying is a form of harrasment which occurs through digital devices like mobile phones, computers and tablets. It mainly happens on platforms like social media, gaming platforms, etc. where people share and view the contents. It is also known as Online bullying. It involves posting hateful comments on each others profiles, spreading rumours, causing embarassment or humiliation by sharing private information about someone else and even lying about someone on social media.

Effects of Cyber Bullying

Mental Health: It can cause an increase in stress and anger which can make a person feel frustrated and embarassed. Sometimes people may suffer through depression or anxiety because of this and might start keeping themselves in isolation along with usage of drugs or alcohol as a medium to escape or reduce the stress and embarassment.

Emotional Health: A person may feel isolated and alone as they are afraid to share any information about themselves with anyone. They may even start losing interest in the things they love overtime.
Once a person is emotionally weak, they can also try to hurt themselves because of all the humiliation they have faced. There may be chances of suicide due to the high/negative effects of cyberbullying as it feels like the best option for them to escape it all.

Physical Health: Due to the impact on emotional health, it can also affect a persons physical health too. The victims of cyberbullying may feel tired all the time (loss of sleep) and even suffer through diseases like depression or sickness, headaches and upset stomach.

Reporting of any kind of bullying is very important

If you are being bullied in any way, the first step is to report or reach out to someone you trust like your parents, relatives or someother trusted adult who can help you.
If you are in school or college, you can reach out to your counsellor or your favourite teacher.
If you feel uncomfortable talking to your closed ones or someone you know, the you can take professional help
If you are being bullied on social media, you should block the account that is bullying you and formally report it on the platform itself.
Reporting about these incidents is necessary and the actions take upon this crime will teach lessons to the bully that such kind of a behaviour is unacceptable.
